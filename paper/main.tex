\begin{abstract}
Action-conditioned JEPA world models (DINO-WM, JEPA-WM, V-JEPA-2-AC) plan well
in free space and fail at contact. We show this is not a generic accuracy gap
but a structural failure we call \emph{Boundary Blindness} (BB): near contact
boundaries --- where small action changes produce qualitatively different
futures --- unimodal latent predictors trained with full-latent $L_2$ collapse
the outcome fan to its mean. We (1)~define a frozen-checkpoint diagnostic that
measures BB against ground-truth outcome spread and show it concentrates at the
pre-grasp boundary on two model families ($\bb$ $1.32/1.28$ at the boundary vs.\
$0.28/0.30$ in free space) and transfers to real-robot DROID data;
(2)~tie BB to planning: at upstream-parity protocol our harness
\emph{reproduces} the published Metaworld Reach baseline ($94\%$ vs.\ reported
$44.8\pm8.9$) yet both the $L_2$ and grounded planners score $0/16$ on push and
pick-and-place --- the arm reaches the goal pose (2--4\,cm) while the object
never moves, the closed-loop signature of BB; (3)~localize the failure by
ablation: mixture predictor heads and metric re-weighting are quantified nulls,
while a 0.5M-parameter grounded dynamics channel $h(z,a)\!\to\!\Delta\text{obj}$
trained on the same cached data restores counterfactual tracking (corr
$0.03\!\to\!0.68$) and halves boundary BB on both models, improving the
closed-loop cost surface (paired distance $+0.089$, CI $[+0.022,+0.162]$)
without yet flipping task success. The bottleneck is the training target, not
the data, the capacity, or the metric.
\end{abstract}

Our contributions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{C1 (diagnostic: CounterfactualBench + Boundary Blindness).}
  A frozen-checkpoint diagnostic suite. Existing action-grounding metrics
  (Counterfactual Ranking Accuracy CRA, Action Usage Gap AUG, Effect-Conditional
  Sensitivity ECS) give the first signal: the models rank a factual action
  against its \emph{opposite} near-perfectly everywhere (CRA $0.96$--$0.99$) but
  collapse against \emph{nearest-neighbour} action distractors, worst at the
  pre-grasp boundary (CRA $0.47$--$0.57$). \emph{Boundary Blindness} (BB), our
  new metric, explains why and localizes it: the rectified gap between the
  world's local action-sensitivity and the model's, on similar-state
  neighbourhoods. BB concentrates at the pre-grasp boundary on both DINO-WM and
  JEPA-WM (CI-aware, per task) and transfers to DROID
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:diagnostic}).
  \item \textbf{C2 (planning link).} At upstream-parity closed-loop protocol,
  planning succeeds exactly where BB is low (Reach: $94\%$/$100\%$, above the
  published $44.8\pm8.9$) and collapses where BB is high (Push, Pick-and-place:
  $0\%$ both arms) with the signature BB predicts: the end-effector arrives,
  the object never moves (Sec.~\ref{sec:planning}).
  \item \textbf{C3 (mechanism + fix).} A controlled fix ladder on frozen
  trunks: mixture heads --- null; metric re-weighting --- null; a probe chain
  showing the object state is \emph{present} in the latent and propagated for
  factual actions but dead for counterfactual ones; and a 0.5M-parameter
  grounded dynamics channel that repairs exactly that channel on both model
  families (Sec.~\ref{sec:fix}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Diagnosis: CounterfactualBench and Boundary Blindness}\label{sec:diagnostic}

We run a frozen-checkpoint diagnostic suite (nothing is trained). It has two
layers: existing action-grounding metrics that establish \emph{that} the models
fail to discriminate nearby actions in contact regimes, and our new Boundary
Blindness metric that explains \emph{why} and pins the failure to the contact
boundary.

\subsection{CounterfactualBench: action-grounding metrics}\label{sec:cfbench}
% metrics/cra.py, aug.py, ecs.py; scripts/05_run_diagnostic.py
For a factual transition $(z_t, a_t, z_{t+1})$ we measure:
\textbf{CRA} (Counterfactual Ranking Accuracy) --- the factual prediction
$F(z_t,a_t)$ should be closer to $z_{t+1}$ than predictions for $K$ distractor
actions $a^-$; we report top-1 accuracy under two distractor families,
\emph{opposite} (the negated action, easy) and \emph{nearest-neighbour}
(the closest other executed action, hard).
\textbf{AUG} (Action Usage Gap) $=\mathbb{E}[\mathrm{MSE}(F(z_t,\pi(a_t)),z_{t+1})
-\mathrm{MSE}(F(z_t,a_t),z_{t+1})]$ with $\pi$ a batch permutation; $\approx 0$
if actions are ignored.
\textbf{ECS} (Effect-Conditional Sensitivity) --- AUG (and CRA) restricted to
transitions that actually move the latent ($\|z_{t+1}-z_t\|>\tau$), so a low
score reflects genuine action-ignoring rather than a near-static step. The
effect-conditioned CRA is the primary signal. CIs are trajectory-clustered
bootstrap.

\noindent The opposite-vs-nearest-neighbour split is the key precursor: the
models are not action-blind in general (opposite CRA $\approx$ 0.97--0.99); they
fail specifically to resolve \emph{small} action differences, and most in the
pre-grasp/contact regimes. CRA alone cannot say whether this is a real boundary
failure or just small one-step latent deltas --- which is what BB resolves.

\subsection{Boundary Blindness: definition}
% metrics/boundary_blindness.py; stratification/boundary_regime.py
For a transition $(z_t, a_t, z_{t+1})$ with a similar-state neighbourhood
$\mathcal{N}(z_t)$ (states within a proprioceptive radius, relaxed to nearest
neighbours), let $S_{\mathrm{true}}$ be the dispersion of \emph{true} outcomes
across $\mathcal{N}$ and $S_{\mathrm{model}}$ the dispersion of the frozen
predictor's outputs for the same (state, action) set. Both are normalized per
task; then
\begin{equation}
  \bb = \mathrm{relu}\big(S_{\mathrm{true}}^{\mathrm{norm}} -
        S_{\mathrm{model}}^{\mathrm{norm}}\big).
\end{equation}
$\bb_{\mathrm{boundary}}$ restricts to transitions whose neighbourhood
straddles an outcome bifurcation (object-displacement label). Unlike CRA, BB
compares the model's action-sensitivity to the \emph{world's} ground-truth
sensitivity, so a high BB cannot be explained away by small one-step deltas.

\section{Localizing the failure: a fix ladder on frozen trunks}\label{sec:fix}

\subsection{What does not work (quantified nulls)}
% (CLAIMS 3.1-3.4)
\textbf{Mixture predictor heads} (soft-NLL / WTA / hard-EM / supervised mode
assignment; $K\in\{2,3\}$; optional state-slice context): every variant beats
its $K{=}1$ control on NLL; none moves $\bb_{\mathrm{boundary}}$. Two measured
causes: (a)~the bifurcation spans only $9.9$ $L_2$ units against a $106$ median
residual --- the latent geometry under-weights the boundary subspace by an
order of magnitude; (b)~the boundary's \emph{action}-dependence is unlearnable
from expert-only data at the head level ($\pi$ action-flip rate $\approx 0$).
\textbf{Metric re-weighting} (probe-subspace $\varphi$-metric): redistributes
BB without reducing it --- one cannot expose a signal the predictor does not
produce.

\subsection{Where the information lives (probe chain)}
% (CLAIMS 3.5)
V1: object state is decodable from the frozen latent (err $0.064$ vs.\ sd
$0.094$). V2: it propagates through the predictor for \emph{factual} actions
($0.059$). V3: the \emph{counterfactual} action$\to$object response is noise
(spread corr $+0.035$). The broken piece is precisely the counterfactual
action$\to$object channel.

\subsection{What works: a grounded dynamics channel}
% (CLAIMS 3.6-3.8)
$h(z_t, a)\!\to\!\Delta\mathrm{obj}$: 0.5M parameters, everything else frozen,
trained on the same cached expert data that the mixture heads failed on.
\begin{table}[h]
\centering\small
\caption{The fix, on both model families (Metaworld; pooled, door-close
excluded).}
\label{tab:fix}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
 & DINO-WM & JEPA-WM \\
\midrule
counterfactual tracking corr (frozen $\to$ $+h$) & $0.035 \to \mathbf{0.682}$ & $0.268^{*} \to \mathbf{0.702}$ \\
$\bb_{\mathrm{boundary}}$ pre-grasp & $1.323 \to \mathbf{0.660}$ ($-50\%$) & $1.280 \to \mathbf{0.620}$ ($-52\%$) \\
boundary-vs-free gap & $1.04 \to 0.32$ & $0.98 \to 0.27$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}\\[2pt]
{\scriptsize $^{*}$probe-only ceiling on JEPA-WM.}
\end{table}

The mixture-null + grounded-success pair revises the natural reading: the
bottleneck is not missing counterfactual \emph{data} (cross-sample
neighbourhood variation suffices) but the \emph{training target} ---
full-latent $L_2$ buries the object's action-dependence.

\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.92\linewidth]{../diagnosis/results/figures/figure_fix_ladder.pdf}
\caption{The fix ladder. (a)~Pooled pre-grasp BB at each rung: mixture heads

($0.66$, $-50\%$). (b)~The probe chain: object state is decodable from the

\subsection{Honest negatives}
% (CLAIMS 3.9, 3.10)
The recipe does not transfer to DROID's noisy whole-state proxy label at 2.1k
transitions (val MSE $0.865$; rank correlation $+0.708$ but spread magnitude
collapsed; BB unchanged): the principle needs a learnable grounding label, which
real-robot datasets currently lack. Open-loop Action Error is no-harm/no-gain
(the metric rewards full-arm expert mimicry); a scale-bug run is disclosed in
the artifact set.
